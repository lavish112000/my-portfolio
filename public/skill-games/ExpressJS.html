The Express.js Café

An interactive guide to the fast, minimalist web framework for Node.js, told through the story of running a café.

1. Setting Up Your Café

Think of Node.js as the empty building for your new café. It provides the basic environment (electricity, water).

Express.js is the entire kitchen staff, equipment, and menu system you install. It provides a simple, organized way to handle customer orders (web requests) and serve them food (web pages and data).

2. Creating Your Menu (Routing)

Routing is like your café's menu. When a customer orders a specific item (a URL path), you have a specific station in the kitchen (a route handler) that prepares it.

3. Your Helpful Staff (Middleware)

Middleware functions are like your café staff who handle an order before it reaches the kitchen. A waiter might log the order time, another might check the customer's age for certain drinks. Each staff member does their job and passes the order to the next person (or `next()` function).

4. The Order & The Food (Req/Res)

Every interaction involves an order slip (the Request object, `req`) and the finished food (the Response object, `res`).
• The `req` object contains the customer's specific requests (e.g., "extra sugar", "no onions").
• The `res` object is what you use to send the finished product back to the customer.
