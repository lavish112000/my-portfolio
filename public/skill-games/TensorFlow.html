How Does It Actually Work?

1. Tensors: The Building Blocks

Tensors are the fundamental data structures. They are multi-dimensional arrays, like lists of numbers, grids of numbers, or even cubes of numbers!

2. Models: The Brains

You assemble layers of "neurons" into a model, or a "neural network". Data flows through, and the model learns patterns.

3. Training: The Learning Process

The model makes a guess, compares it to the correct answer (calculates "loss"), and adjusts its neurons slightly to get better. This loop is repeated thousands of times.

Real-Life Scenario: House Price Prediction

Let's gamify this! Imagine you're a real estate mogul. You want to build a model to predict house prices based on size.

Your Prediction Challenge

Drag the slider to input the size of a house (in sq. ft.) and our simple TensorFlow model will predict its price.

How it works:

  1. Input Data: We trained a model on thousands of houses with their size (input) and price (output).
  2. Finding a Pattern: The model learned a simple relationship: price ≈ size * C (where C is a constant it figured out).
  3. Prediction: When you provide a size, it uses this learned formula to make an educated guess on the price. This is a basic form of linear regression!
